ABL's mission is to provide rockets for clients who need low cost, agile launches with no frills. With my work I've tried to convey that DIY, rough and tumble attitude, while evoking the ingenuinty and technical scale of the Apollo Missions. As a freelance designer and illustrator, I've created modular assets for use in marketing and internal presentations. My work here is ongoing, but I've made 3D models, illustrations, maps, and more.

The RS-1, ABL Space System's flagship rocket, has three form-factors. I began with simple line illustrations of these. It was important to me that the turnaround on different angles of these rockets, particularly the Medium and Heavy, which are still in production, was fast and accurate, so all line illustrations began with me 3D modeling in Blender and were procedurally generated from there.

This diagram depicts the modularity of a typical RS-1 launch. The rocket and all auxiliary equiptment can be shipped in iso containers and the launch can be controlled remotely.

These are detailed exploded views of the various stages of each of the rockets that show, with a little artistic license, how the rocket components would separate. These are also generated from 3D models I created.

The first and second stages of ABL rockets are retrieved by tugboats after their reentry and water landings. This is a simple diagram depicting those stages and their reentry. For this diagram I made a both flat and isometric versions.

This series of illustrations depicts the modular shipping of rocket parts in iso shipping containers and is to-scale.

This series of illustrations was inspired by the speculative illustration commissioned by NASA for the Apollo missions. I've attempted here to evoke the airbrushed texture of a mid-century illustration with 3D shaders and minor post processing.
